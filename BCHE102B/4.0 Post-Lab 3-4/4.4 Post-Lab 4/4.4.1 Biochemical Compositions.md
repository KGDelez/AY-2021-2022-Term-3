## Approximate chemical composition of a cell
|                          | E.coli | S. cerevisiae | Mammalian cell |
| ------------------------ | ------ | ------------- | -------------- |
| % total weight **Water** | 70     | 80            | 70             |
| % dry weight **DNA**     | 3      | 0.1-0.6       | 1              |
| **RNA**                  | 20     | 6-12          | 4              |
| **Proteins**             | 50-55  | 35-60         | 60             |
| **Lipids**               | 7-9    | 4-10          | 13               |
- Biomolecules make up a big percentage of the body that isnt water
	- because biomolecules have lots of functions
- proteins are based on the DNA
	- **DNA** --Transcription-> **RNA** -- Translation-> **Protein**

- centrifugation separates cell components on the basis of size and density
	- larger and denser components move to the bottom
	- smaller, less dense components suspend above - supernatant
	- cell homogenate --(low speed centrifugation)-> whole cells, nuclei, cytoskeletons (pellet 1)
		- supernatant 1 --(medium-speed centrifugation)-> mitochondria, lysosomes, peroxisomes (pellet 2)
			- supernatant 2 --(high-speed centrifugation)-> microsomes, other small vesicles (pellet 3)
				- supernatant 3 --(very high-speed centrifugation) -> ribosomes, viruses, large macromolecules (pellet 4)
